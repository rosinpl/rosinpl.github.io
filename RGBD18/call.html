workshop in conjunction with ACCV 2018 on

RGB-D - sensing and understanding via combined colour and depth
Home Call for papers Submission People Invited Speakers Program

RGB-D images capture both photometric and geometric information, and over the last few years the cost of RGB-D sensors has dropped while their accuracy has increased. RGB-D sensors and analytics is a timely topic, as RGB-D data is now widespread and has been incorporated into many applications, such as health care, robotics, fashion, metrology, vehicular technology.

However, data is still typically noisy and incomplete, data processing techniques still lag behind traditional image modalities, real-time processing of data becomes more challenging as data volumes increase. Therefore, many research challenges remain.

The benefit of this workshop will be to provide the opportunity for people from disparate areas such as computer vision, robotics, graphics, as well as application areas such as healthcare and creative industries, to exchange ideas.

Topics of interest include

  • Acquisition of RGBD data
  • Joint analysis of colour and depth
  • Processing of temporal RGBD data
  • Recognition from RGBD data, e.g. human activity detection, object detection, event detection
  • RGBD salient object detection
  • Robust processing of RGBD data
  • Real-time tracking from RGBD data
  • Segmentation of RGBD data
  • Extracting features from RGBD data
  • Geometric and semantic modelling from RGBD data
  • 3D reconstruction from RGBD data
  • RGBD SLAM
  • The evaluation of RGBD systems & datasets
  • Applications of RGBD systems
  • RGBD LiDAR processing
