Some History

I gained the B.Sc. degree in Computer Science and Microprocessor Systems in 1984 from Strathclyde University, Glasgow, and the Ph.D. degree in Information Engineering from City University, London in 1988. I was a research fellow at City University, developing a prototype system for the Home Office to detect and classify intruders in image sequences. I worked on the Alvey project "Model-Based Interpretation of Radiological Images" at Guy's Hospital, London before becoming a lecturer at the Department of Computer Science, Curtin University of Technology, Perth, Australia. I moved to Italy to work at the Institute for Remote Sensing Applications at the Joint Research Centre. followed by a return to the UK, becoming lecturer at the Department of Information Systems and Computing, Brunel University, London, UK. At the beginning of 2000 I moved to the School of Computer Science & Informatics, Cardiff University.

Software

Some software that I have developed (some of it in collaboration with colleagues) is available for:
  • Curves
    • approximating curves by lines and arcs
    • multi-scale segmentation and matching of curves using codons
    • automatic scale selection for curve smoothing
    • fitting ellipses
    • fitting superellipses
    • computing convexity and rectilinearity shape measures
    • a convexity measure for partial boundaries (one or more open curves)
    • a "robust" convexity measure
    • computing linearity of curves
  • Images
    • automatic thresholding of edges
    • unimodal thresholding for edge maps and many other types of image
    • circular thresholding (for circular quantities such as hue, orientation, etc.)
    • thresholding for change detection (difference thresholding)
    • salience distance transform
    • salient region detection
    • computing disconnectedness of multiple component shapes
See also software by colleagues for our joint work:
  • Images
    • face geometry and appearance editing: DeepFaceEditing
    • artistic line drawings of faces: APDrawingGAN (original CVPR 2019 version)
    • artistic line drawings of faces: APDrawingGAN2 (improved PAMI 2020 version)
    • artistic line drawings of faces: unpaired training version (original CVPR 2020 version)
    • artistic line drawings of faces: QMUPD (improved PAMI 2022 version)
    • structure-preserving neural style transfer
    • superpixels: FLIC
    • subspace clustering: autoSC
    • subspace clustering: good neighbors
    • dynamic match kernel for image retrieval
    • weakly supervised sentiment analysis (WSCNet)
    • detection of affective regions
    • segmentation of people
    • seam carving (i.e. image resizing)
    • single image super-resolution: MS-RHDN
    • perceptual metric for facial sketches: Scoot
    • binarized normed gradients for objectness estimation at 300fps: BING
    • virtual try-on of clothing: CP-VTON+
    • multi-modal image fusion: AMM-FuseNet
    • hair editing using text and images: HairManip
    • universal domain adaptation: UniDA
    • synthesis of scalable vector graphics from images: SuperSVG
    • assessment of image aesthetics: BAID
  • Video
    • animating portrait line drawings
  • Meshes
    • mesh filtering
    • saliency of 3D meshes ("Mesh Saliency via Spectral Processing", ACM TOG, 2014)
    • salience of 3D meshes ("Mesh Saliency via Weakly Supervised Classification-for-Saliency CNN", IEEE TVCG, 2021)
    • saliency of 3D meshes ("3D Visual Saliency: An Independent Perceptual Measure or A Derivative of 2D Image Saliency?", IEEE PAMI, 2023)
    • distinction of 3D meshes
    • automatic unpaired shape deformation transfer
    • correspondence coverage measure for 3D shape correspondence and registration methods
    • skeleton-based canonical forms for non-rigid 3D shape retrieval
    • learning on 3D Meshes with Laplacian encoding and pooling (mesh segmentation and classification)
    • shape complexity
